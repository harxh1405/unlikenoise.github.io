About us

Welcome to the Unlike Noise Community

"We're dedicated to raising awareness and taking action against the often-overlooked issue of noise pollution in our communities. Here, we recognize that noise pollution is not just an inconvenience; it's a serious environmental and public health concern that affects millions of people worldwide. From bustling urban areas to tranquil rural settings, noise pollution can disrupt sleep, increase stress levels, and even lead to long-term health problems. Our mission is to educate individuals and communities about the sources and impacts of noise pollution while advocating for effective solutions to mitigate its effects. Through advocacy, outreach, and community engagement, we strive to create a quieter, healthier, and more peaceful world for all.Join us in our mission to silence the noise and create a world where everyone can enjoy the benefits of peace and quiet. Together, we can make a positive difference for our planet and future generations. Let's turn down the volume and turn up the conversation about noise pollution."

Recent Events

Recent Event

"Voice Against Noise"


Representatives from various localities of Pune have joined forces to sensitise government officials on the menace of noise pollution — from an increase in the number of pubs in residential areas to construction and traffic.

Recent Event

"Horn Not Ok Please"


In a bid to sensitise motorists to reduce use of horn in the city, members of the Confederation of Indian Industry (CII-Young Indians) have ventured into a campaign with a slogan ‘Horn Not Ok Please’.Starting the campaign in association with the Indian Oil Corporation, the members pasted stickers on vehicles entering petrol stations.

Anti-Honking Drive


The Fight to Curb a Health Scourge in India: Noise Pollution.An anti-honking sign hangs on the wall at the Earth Saviours Foundation just outside New Delhi. The foundation is active in caring for the homeless and several environmental causes, including noise pollution.

Recent Event

"Chilla Matt"campaign


The campaign by 4 media students Raina Saxena, Iesha Chaudhari, Kantesh Keswani and Harshida Shah of Thadomal Shahani Centre for Media and Communication – TSCMC, involved a sustained social media campaign along with on ground activities involving the youth to sensitize them about no honking and other aspects of noise pollution.
